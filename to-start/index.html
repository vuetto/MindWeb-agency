Here’s Why I Started The

“Twelve Olympians”...

Hi, my name is Ugo.
 
It was a night of summer 2016. It’s around 5pm. I’m getting off my room to sit on my black couch, next to my father. 

I felt dead inside this day. At the time, I felt like I had no purpose.

I started a « what’s the weather like » conversation with my father... Until he shared something.

I don’t remember precisely what he said but I was surprised. Because it was the first time he shared something with that much passion.

He shared how he felt in love with Nepal and how he wanted to create a non-profit to help the country.

For some reason, I felt that to run a non-profit, we needed to have a website. So I said « but… You don’t have a website? »

I waited a second… And before he answered, I said with a shy voice « Let… Let me do it. »

I don’t remember why I said that. But I felt like I needed something to challenge myself.

I remember that day like it was yesterday because I remember saying to myself « what if I don’t have to follow the rules? »

I spent a few minutes googling « how to create a website », found a website called CodeCademy, and started the first lesson.

Two days later, the website was created and online. 

That day of summer 2016, as I was 16… The Twelve Olympians was born. 

Since then, I help companies to scale online without letting go of their identity.

The "Twelve Olympians" is a celebration for those who - just like me - said to themselves « What If I Don't Have To Follow The Rules? »

Because together, we're stronger... And we don't have to follow those rules.

Ugo

After Zeus and his brothers and sisters defeated the Titans, they ruled the universe. Zeus became the king of the gods because of his wisdom and strength and each of the Twelve Olympians, Zeus’ brothers and sisters (minus Hades) and six of his children, also became gods and each had a different duty. They resided on Mount Olympus, a mountain that reached up into the clouds towards the heavens where they looked down on Earth."

The Twelve Olympians is a metaphor for the 12 businesses. All of them have fought to get to where they are, and none of them is going to stop before reaching the clouds. People come and go, but the spirit of the Gods never die.
